The Problem

Earnings calls contain signals
hidden in 60+ pages of text

  • Analysts manually read thousands of transcripts per quarter
  • Subtle language shifts predict stock movement but humans miss them
  • Management tone vs analyst tone divergence is the #1 return predictor
  • Risk signals are buried across prepared remarks and Q&A sections
RESEARCH FINDING
Raw sentiment scores are statistically insignificant for predicting returns (p > 0.5). What matters is momentum (QoQ change), analyst sentiment (p < 0.001), and prepared vs Q&A divergence (p = 0.002).

Sentiment Analysis

Three Models, One Story

Loughran-McDonald
Finance-specific dictionary built from 10-K filings. Catches formal language like impairment, restructuring, litigation.
Lexicon-basedFinance-tuned
VADER
Rule-based scorer for general language. Handles punctuation, capitalization, and everyday expressions that LM misses.
Rule-basedGeneral NLP
FinBERT
Transformer neural network fine-tuned on financial news. Understands context—"not bad" = positive, "growth slowed" = negative.
Deep LearningContextual
Key Insight: We go beyond raw scores. The dashboard shows Management vs Analyst gap (overoptimism signal), Prepared vs Q&A shift (credibility under pressure), and quarter-over-quarter momentum (strongest return predictor).

Risk Detection

Keyword + Semantic RAG Detection

Two-layer detection system combining traditional keyword matching with embedding-based semantic similarity to catch risks expressed in novel language.
Keyword Layer
255 financial risk terms with negation handling, severity scoring (high / medium / low), and context awareness
Semantic Layer (RAG)
Embedding similarity to risk anchor sentences with differential scoring—catches risks keywords miss
10 Risk Categories
DemandMargin Supply ChainRegulatory CompetitionFX / Macro LiquidityTech Execution LaborGeopolitical
RAG Value-Add: Semantic detection finds risks like "Our pipeline is weaker than expected"—no explicit risk keyword, but the embedding matches the demand risk anchor. Each detection shows the matched anchor sentence for full explainability.
